2d Case

First, let's consider a system of two linear equations with two unknowns. Namely, \(2x-y=0\) and \(-x+2y=3\) . We can represent these equations graphically as:

This same set of equations may be represented as one matrix form linear algebra equation represented generally as \(A\overrightarrow{x}=\overrightarrow{b}\) or according to our example as: $$ \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ y \end{array} \right]= \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] $$ The column vector representation of the same linear algebra equation is: $$ x\left[ \begin{array}{c} 2 \\ -1\end{array} \right] + y\left[ \begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array} \right]= \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] $$ The column vector form expresses the right hand vector solution as a linear combination of the columns shown in the original linear algebra equation.
