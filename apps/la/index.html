A segway into linear algebra:

Let's have some fun with linear algebra. First, let's consider a system of two linear equations with two unknowns. Namely, $2x-y=0$ and $-x+2y=3$. We can represent these equations graphically as:

This same set of equations may be represented as one linear algebra equation of the form $A\overrightharpup{x}=\overrightharpup{b}$ such as shown below. $$ \left[ \begin{array}{cc} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} x \\ y \end{array} \right]= \left[ \begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 3 \end{array} \right] $$
